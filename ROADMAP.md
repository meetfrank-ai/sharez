# Sharez — Product & Revenue Roadmap

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (months 1-3)
### Goal: Get the core product working and seed with creators

**Build**
- User auth (email + Google OAuth)
- Portfolio import: screenshot with AI extraction + manual entry + XLSX transaction upload
- Holdings display with percentage weights (no rand amounts ever)
- Notes system: create, tag to stocks, set visibility (public / inner circle / vault)
- Basic feed: chronological timeline of notes and trade shares
- User profiles: portfolio pie chart, holdings list, notes, bio
- Follow system with approval (for inner circle tier)
- Stock pages: basic info pulled from EODHD or yfinance, community notes for that stock
- Proof of trade: screenshot upload, AI extraction, verified trade card in feed

**Seed**
- Personally recruit 10-20 SA investing creators (Twitter/X, TikTok, YouTube finfluencers)
- Help them import portfolios and publish their first notes
- Get their feedback, iterate on the product
- Creators share their Sharez profiles on their existing channels

**Revenue: R0**
- No monetisation yet. Focus on product and user acquisition.

**Key metrics to hit**
- 10-20 active creators with imported portfolios
- 1,000-2,000 signed up users
- 50+ notes published

---

## Phase 2: Social growth (months 3-6)
### Goal: Build the community flywheel and launch vault

**Build**
- Vault subscription system: creator sets price, users pay monthly
- Payment integration (PayFast or Yoco for ZAR payments)
- Sharez takes 15-20% commission on vault subscriptions
- AI stock summary cards: per-stock page with AI-generated summary, sentiment pills, community data, risk note, news digest
- AI investing style labels on creator profiles
- Activity alerts: notify followers when a creator shares a trade
- Community bar on stock pages: "14 people you follow hold this stock"
- Sector breakdown (ISIN to EODHD lookup)
- Search and discovery: find creators by investing style, top holdings, return %

**Seed**
- Open to public sign-up
- Creators drive organic sign-ups by sharing profiles on social media
- Weekly "most held stocks on Sharez" content published by the platform account
- Begin building relationships with ETF providers (introductory emails, share early platform data)

**Revenue: R10-50K/month**
- Early vault subscriptions from creator followers
- Expect 5-10 creators with vaults, averaging R200/month, 20-50 subscribers each
- Sharez commission: 15-20% of vault GMV

**Key metrics to hit**
- 50+ active creators
- 5,000-10,000 users
- 5-10 active vault creators
- 200+ vault subscribers total

## Phase 3: ETF partnerships (months 6-12)
### Goal: Launch the first revenue layer that scales independently of user payments

**Build — Sponsored education modules**
- "Learn" section in the app with short educational modules
- ETF providers sponsor modules: "Understanding index investing" by Satrix, "Offshore exposure explained" by Sygnia
- Modules appear in feed, on relevant stock pages, and in new user onboarding
- Track completions and engagement per module
- Optional: "Learn and Earn" where users get a small reward (R5-20) for completing a module, funded by the sponsor

**Build — Data insights dashboard (internal first)**
- Aggregate anonymised platform data: most held stocks/ETFs, allocation trends, net inflows/outflows
- Weekly/monthly reports showing retail investor behaviour
- Initially use this data in partnership pitches ("here's what our users hold — your product is #3")
- Later: formalise as a paid data product

**Build — Creator sponsorship marketplace**
- ETF providers can browse verified creators on the platform
- Filter by: investing style, follower count, most held products, engagement rate
- Sponsor a creator to write a note about their product
- Note is clearly labelled "Sponsored by [provider]" but written in the creator's voice
- Creator sets their own sponsorship pricing
- Sharez takes 20% platform fee on all sponsorship deals
- Full transparency: the creator's portfolio shows whether they actually hold the sponsored product

**Sell**
- Approach Satrix, Sygnia, CoreShares, 10X with the pitch:
  - "We have X thousand verified SA retail investors actively discussing ETFs"
  - "Here's anonymised data on what they hold — your product vs competitors"
  - "We offer three partnership products: education modules, data reports, creator sponsorships"
- Start with one pilot partner at a reduced rate to prove the model
- Use the pilot results to sign additional providers

**Revenue: R100-300K/month**
- Vault commissions growing: R50-80K/month
- Sponsored education: 2-3 providers at R30-50K/month each = R60-150K/month
- Creator sponsorship marketplace: early stage, R20-40K/month in platform fees
- Data reports: first quarterly report sold, R50-100K

**Key metrics to hit**
- 20,000+ users
- 100+ active creators
- 30+ vault creators
- 2-3 ETF provider partnerships signed
- First sponsored creator notes published

## Phase 4: Broker integrations (months 12-18)
### Goal: Add auto-linking for power users and expand the verified creator base

**Build — SnapTrade integration for IBKR**
- SnapTrade provides read-only access to Interactive Brokers accounts
- Auto-sync holdings, transactions, and balances
- Real-time trade detection: when a creator buys/sells on IBKR, it auto-posts to their feed
- "IBKR linked" verification badge — strongest trust signal on the platform
- This becomes the premium creator experience

**Build — Saxo Bank direct API integration**
- Saxo has a full OpenAPI with OAuth
- Similar auto-sync experience to IBKR
- "Saxo linked" verification badge

**Build — Verification tier system**
- IBKR/Saxo linked = "Auto-verified" (green badge, highest trust)
- EE screenshot import = "Screenshot verified" (blue badge, medium trust)
- Manual entry = no badge

**Build — AI portfolio features**
- AI portfolio comparison (vault): subscriber vs creator side-by-side
- "What would they do?" feature: ask a question about a stock, get AI answer based on creator's history and style
- AI-generated weekly portfolio digest for vault subscribers

**Revenue: R300-600K/month**
- Vault commissions: R150-250K/month (growing creator base, higher-priced vaults)
- ETF partnerships: R100-200K/month (3-5 providers)
- Creator sponsorships: R50-100K/month in platform fees
- Data reports: R50-100K/month

**Key metrics to hit**
- 50,000+ users
- 200+ active creators
- 50+ creators with IBKR or Saxo auto-linked
- 5+ ETF provider partnerships
- Creator sponsorship marketplace active with regular deal flow

### Goal: Become the default platform for SA investing content and community

- Advanced portfolio analytics: sector exposure, geographic breakdown, benchmark comparison
- AI portfolio coaching: personalised suggestions based on holdings
- Tax reporting tools for SA investors (CGT calculations, dividend tax tracking)
- Priced at R49-99/month for users

**Build — Creator tools**
- Creator analytics dashboard: views, followers, engagement, vault revenue
- Scheduled notes: write and queue notes for future publishing
- Content templates: earnings reaction, stock deep-dive, portfolio update

**Build — Platform expansion**
- Web app (full feature parity with mobile)
- Explore additional broker integrations based on user demand
- Consider other African markets (Nigeria, Kenya — growing retail investing scenes)

**Revenue: R600K-1.5M/month**
- Vault commissions: R300-500K/month
- ETF partnerships: R200-400K/month (5-8 providers, expanded product suite)
- Creator sponsorships: R100-200K/month
- Premium user subscriptions: R50-150K/month
- Data products: R100-200K/month

**Key metrics to hit**
- 100,000-150,000 users
- 300+ active creators
- R18M+ annual revenue run rate

### How each ETF partnership product works

**Sponsored education**
- ETF provider pays Sharez a monthly fee (R30-80K/month)
- Sharez creates or co-creates educational content modules
- Content lives natively in the app: feed, stock pages, onboarding
- Labelled "Sponsored by [provider]" — transparent, not hidden
- Metrics provided to sponsor: completions, time spent, click-throughs
- Optional "Learn and Earn": small cash rewards to users for completing modules, funded by sponsor

**Data insights**
- Quarterly reports: R50-150K per report per provider
- Anonymised, aggregated platform data
- Contents: most held ETFs ranked, net allocation changes, sector trend shifts, demographic breakdowns
- Comparison data: "your ETF vs top 3 competitors in retail holdings"
- Data is credible because portfolios are verified (screenshot + broker linking)
- As user base grows, data becomes more statistically significant and more valuable

**Creator sponsorship marketplace**
- ETF provider browses verified creators on Sharez
- Filters: follower count, investing style, which products they hold, engagement rate
- Provider offers a sponsorship deal: R5,000-R20,000 per note
- Creator writes a note about the product in their own voice
- Note is tagged "Sponsored by [provider]" — full transparency
- Creator's portfolio is visible — followers can see if they actually hold the product
- Sharez takes 20% platform fee
- Provider gets: engagement metrics, reach data, conversion tracking (did readers add the ETF to their portfolio?)

**Why this is better than traditional influencer marketing:**
1. Verified portfolios — the creator can't fake holding the product
2. Targeted audience — every follower is a verified investor, not a random social media user
3. Measurable — you can track from note → profile view → portfolio addition
4. Native — the content lives where investment decisions are made, not on YouTube or Instagram
5. Persistent — notes stay on the stock page forever, not buried in a social feed

## Key Risks and Mitigations by Phase

| Phase | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Creators don't adopt | Pre-seed with known finfluencers. Make it easy to import and publish. |
| 2 | Users sign up but don't engage | Strong onboarding: prompt to follow 5 creators, show the best notes immediately. |
| 2 | Vault pricing too high for SA market | Let the market decide. Some creators at R99, others at R2000. Range is the feature. |
| 3 | ETF providers don't see value | Start with one pilot at reduced rate. Use pilot data to prove ROI. |
| 3 | Regulatory concerns about sponsored content | Clear "Sponsored by" labelling. Legal review. FSCA guidance on what constitutes financial advice vs opinion. |
| 4 | SnapTrade doesn't support enough SA brokers | IBKR alone is sufficient for power creators. Screenshot/XLSX covers the rest. |
| 5 | Blossom expands to SA | Unlikely (no SA broker support). If they do, your local focus, ZAR context, and creator monetisation are moats they'd take years to replicate. |
